What Is a Daily Interest Calculator?

A daily interest calculator tells you exactly how much interest accrues on a sum of money for each day it sits in a loan, savings account, fixed deposit, or investment. Instead of waiting for your monthly bank statement, you get a clear, day-by-day picture of how interest builds — whether you're a borrower trying to understand costs or a saver watching your money grow.

Most financial institutions calculate interest on a daily basis, even if they only show you the figure monthly. That means the numbers are moving every single day. Our calculator brings those hidden daily movements to the surface.

Simple Interest vs. Compound Interest

Simple Interest

Simple interest is the most straightforward method. You earn (or owe) interest only on the original principal — the amount never changes as a base. It's commonly used for short-term loans, personal loans, car loans, and some government bonds.

Formula: Interest = Principal × Rate × Time (in years)

Example: ₹10,000 at 8% annual rate for 90 days
Daily interest = ₹10,000 × 8% ÷ 365 = ₹2.19 per day
Total interest for 90 days = ₹197.26

Compound Interest

Compound interest is where the real power of money shows up. Here, interest is added to the principal at regular intervals — daily, monthly, or annually — and then that larger amount starts earning interest too. Over time, this creates exponential growth (or exponential debt).

Formula: A = P × (1 + r/n)^(n×t)

Savings accounts, fixed deposits, mutual funds, and most investment vehicles use compound interest. The more frequently it compounds, the faster your money grows.

Tip: Even a difference of 0.5% in annual interest rate can translate to thousands of dollars over a few years thanks to compounding.

How to Use This Calculator

Using the daily interest calculator takes less than 30 seconds:

  1. Enter your principal — This is the starting amount. For a loan, it's what you borrowed. For savings, it's what you deposited.
  2. Enter the annual interest rate — Use the rate shown on your loan agreement or bank account. Enter it as a percentage (e.g., 7.5 for 7.5%).
  3. Enter the number of days — How many days does the interest period cover? You can also pick a start and end date and let the calculator count the days automatically.
  4. Choose your mode — Select Simple Interest for straightforward calculations, or Compound Interest and choose how often it compounds (daily, monthly, quarterly, etc.).
  5. Click Calculate — Instantly see your daily interest, total interest, final amount, and a full period breakdown.

Who Uses a Daily Interest Calculator?

Borrowers

Knowing your daily interest cost helps you plan prepayments, avoid penalties, and understand exactly what you're paying for. Even a single extra payment can cut days or weeks of interest.

Savers & Investors

Comparing accounts? A fixed deposit vs. a recurring deposit? Use this calculator to see which one actually puts more money in your pocket, factoring in compounding frequency.

Businesses

Short-term loans and lines of credit often charge daily interest. Finance managers use daily calculations to manage cash flow and forecast costs.

Legal & Accounting

Calculating interest on overdue payments, court judgments, or tax liabilities often requires precise daily interest figures.

Key Terms Explained

Principal
The original sum of money on which interest is calculated. In a loan, it's the amount you borrowed. In savings, it's your initial deposit.
Annual Interest Rate (APR)
The yearly percentage rate charged or earned. Our calculator converts annual rates to daily equivalents automatically.
Compounding Frequency
How often accumulated interest is added to your principal (Daily, Monthly, Quarterly, or Annually).
Total Amount
Principal plus all interest accrued over the period. This is the amount you'd receive at maturity or owe at the end.

Tips to Reduce Interest Costs

  • Make early or extra payments. Every rupee you pay back early cuts the base on which interest is calculated.
  • Negotiate your rate. Even a 0.5% reduction can save hundreds of rupees over a single year.
  • Choose daily compounding for savings, not loans. Earn more on savings, but avoid it when borrowing if possible.
  • Watch the number of days. Confirm if your lender uses 360 or 365 days, as it affects the effective daily rate.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is daily interest calculated?

Daily interest is calculated by dividing your annual interest rate by 365 and multiplying by your principal. For simple interest: Daily Interest = Principal × (Annual Rate ÷ 365).

Can I use this for credit card interest?

Yes. Most credit cards compound interest daily. Enter your balance as principal, APR as annual rate, and select Compound Interest with Daily frequency.

Does early loan repayment save money?

Absolutely. Since interest accrues daily on the remaining principal, every extra payment reduces your interest charges the very next day.

What is the Rule of 72?

Divide 72 by your annual interest rate to estimate how many years it takes for your money to double with compound interest. At 6%, it takes about 12 years.
