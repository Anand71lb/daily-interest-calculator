What is a Daily Interest Calculator?

A Daily Interest Calculator is a tool used to determine the interest earned on regular daily contributions to a savings scheme. Popular in India through Recurring Deposit (RD) accounts, Post Office schemes, and chit funds, this calculation method helps you understand returns when you save a fixed amount every day.

Unlike lump sum investments, daily deposits build your corpus gradually. The Average Days Method fairly calculates interest by considering that your first deposit earns interest for the entire period, while your last deposit earns for just one day.

How This Daily Interest Calculator Works

The Daily Interest Calculator uses the Average Days Method, which is the standard for Recurring Deposits in India. Here's the step-by-step process:

Interest = Total Deposited √ó (Rate / 100) √ó (Average Days / 365)

Understanding the Formula

  • Total Deposited: Daily Amount √ó Number of Days (e.g., ‚Çπ1000 √ó 365 = ‚Çπ3,65,000)
  • Average Days: (Total Days + 1) / 2 (e.g., (365 + 1) / 2 = 183 days)
  • Rate: Annual interest rate in percentage
  • 365: Days in a year for accurate calculation

Why Average Days?

The Average Days Method accounts for the staggered nature of deposits. Your first deposit earns interest for all 365 days, the second for 364 days, and so on. The mathematical average works out to (n+1)/2 days, which simplifies the calculation while maintaining accuracy.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. How does our Daily Interest Calculator work?

This Daily Interest Calculator uses the Average Days Method. The total deposited amount is multiplied by the annual interest rate and average deposit period ((Total Days + 1) / 2), then divided by 365. For example, ‚Çπ1000/day for 365 days at 6.5% earns ‚Çπ11,878 interest.

2. What is the Average Days Method for RD calculation?

The Average Days Method recognizes that not all deposits earn interest for the same duration. Your first deposit earns for the full period (say 365 days), the second for 364 days, and so on. The average is calculated as (n+1)/2 days. For 365 days: (365+1)/2 = 183 average days. This is the standard method used by Indian banks and Post Office.

3. Can I use this for Post Office Daily Interest?

Absolutely! This Daily Interest Calculator is perfect for Post Office Recurring Deposits. Enter your monthly deposit as daily (e.g., ‚Çπ10,000/month = ‚Çπ333/day for 30-day months), select 12 months or 365 days, and use 6.5% rate. The Average Days Method matches Post Office calculation standards.

4. What's the difference between daily deposit and lump sum FD?

Daily Deposits (RD): You invest gradually (e.g., ‚Çπ1000/day) and interest is calculated using average days. Fixed Deposit (FD): One-time lump sum investment where entire amount earns interest for full tenure. FDs typically yield higher absolute interest as the full principal works from day one, but RDs are better for building saving discipline.

5. Is daily deposit better than monthly RD?

Both earn the same interest using Average Days Method. The choice depends on your income pattern. Daily deposits suit daily wage earners, shopkeepers, and those who prefer micro-commitments. Monthly RD suits salaried individuals and is more convenient. Interest-wise, ‚Çπ1000 daily for 30 days = ‚Çπ30,000 monthly RD.

At 6.5% annual interest for 365 days:

  • Total Deposited: ‚Çπ500 √ó 365 = ‚Çπ1,82,500

7. Which bank gives highest RD interest rate?

As of 2026, small finance banks like Ujjivan, Equitas, and AU offer 7.5-8% for RDs, higher than commercial banks (6-7%). Post Office offers competitive 6.5% with guaranteed government backing. Always compare tenure-specific rates and verify RBI deposit insurance coverage before investing.

8. Can I withdraw RD before maturity?

Yes, but with penalties. Most banks allow premature withdrawal after 6 months of deposits, deducting 1-2% from the applicable interest rate. Post Office RDs can be closed after 3 years without penalty. Check your scheme's specific terms before opening the account.

9. Is RD interest taxable in India?

Yes, RD interest is fully taxable under "Income from Other Sources" as per your income tax slab. Banks deduct TDS if annual interest exceeds ‚Çπ40,000 (‚Çπ50,000 for senior citizens). Submit Form 15G/15H if your total income is below taxable limit to avoid TDS deduction.

10. What happens if I miss a daily deposit?

Most RD schemes allow some flexibility with grace periods. However, repeated defaults can lead to penalties (‚Çπ1-2 per ‚Çπ100 per month) or account closure. For daily deposits, consider automating through standing instructions or using digital apps that auto-debit from your bank account.

11. Can I have multiple RD accounts?

Yes! You can open multiple RD accounts in the same bank or across different banks. This helps diversify tenure (some short-term, some long-term) and take advantage of different interest rates. No legal limit on number of RD accounts.

12. Is this calculator accurate for all banks?

Yes, this calculator uses the standard Average Days Method prescribed by RBI and followed by all Indian banks and Post Office. However, some banks compound quarterly vs annually - for precise calculations including quarterly compounding, verify with your bank's RD calculator or interest computation method.
